4 year cycle

The four-year Bitcoin cycle is a pattern of Bitcoin price movements that has been observed since the cryptocurrency's inception in 2009. The cycle is characterized by four distinct phases: accumulation, bull market, bear market, and reset.
During the accumulation phase, Bitcoin prices remain relatively low and stable, allowing investors to accumulate coins at a low cost.
This is followed by a bull market, where prices rise rapidly as more investors enter the market. After the bull market peaks, a bear market ensues.
